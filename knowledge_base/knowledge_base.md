# Knowledge Base - Glass Bottle Quality Control

## DEFECT CATALOG

### Broken Large
Keywords: large crack, broken top, broken rim, broken glass, large hole, missing glass, broken piece
Description: A large structural break on the top opening of the glass bottle. Characterized by missing glass fragments, large cracks, or significant portions of the rim broken off. The bottle opening may appear deformed or collapsed.
Causes: mechanical impact during production or transport, excessive pressure on the rim, thermal shock during manufacturing.
Severity: High

### Broken Small
Keywords: small crack, chip, small broken piece, minor crack, small hole, broken part
Description: A small crack or chip on the top opening of the glass bottle. The structural integrity is partially compromised but no large fragments are missing. May appear as a thin fracture line or a small chip on the rim edge.
Causes: minor mechanical stress, handling damage, micro-fractures from production.
Severity: Medium

### Contamination
Keywords: contamination, foreign material, fabric, string, hair, stain, dirty, scratch, black mark, brown streak, substance, object inside, paper, dust, particles
Description: Foreign material or surface damage visible on or inside the top opening of the bottle. Includes physical objects such as threads, hairs, fabric pieces, paper fragments stuck inside or on the rim. Also includes surface damage such as scratches, stains, discoloration, or dark marks on the glass surface.
Causes: production line contamination, improper handling, environmental exposure, inadequate cleaning procedures.
Severity: Medium to High depending on contamination type and extent.

---

## CORRECTIVE ACTIONS

### Broken Large
- Isolate the defective unit immediately — do not fill or use
- Quarantine the entire production batch pending inspection
- Inspect at least 20 adjacent units from the same production line
- Check production line for mechanical stress, impact damage, or pressure anomalies
- Notify quality control manager immediately
- Document defect with image and anomaly score

### Broken Small
- Isolate the defective unit — do not fill or use
- Inspect 10 adjacent units from the same production line
- Check handling procedures and conveyor belt settings
- Review temperature settings to rule out thermal stress
- Flag batch for secondary manual inspection

### Contamination
- Isolate the defective unit immediately
- Identify contamination source: physical object, surface stain, or scratch
- If physical object (fabric, string, hair): inspect entire batch, review production line cleanliness
- If surface damage (scratch, stain): inspect 10 adjacent units, review handling procedures
- Clean and inspect production line if contamination appears systemic
- Review supplier quality for raw glass material
